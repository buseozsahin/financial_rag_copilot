NVIDIA originally focused on gaming GPUs, but gaming is now a small part of its business compared to data centers.

NVIDIA generates the majority of its revenue from its Data Center segment, which is driven by AI infrastructure demand.

NVIDIA's Data Center business accounts for roughly 90% of total revenue, making it the dominant revenue source.

NVIDIA’s major customers include large cloud providers such as Microsoft, Amazon, and Google.

NVIDIA also operates in gaming, professional visualization, and automotive/robotics segments.

NVIDIA is expanding into robotics, autonomous vehicles, and telecom networks as part of its long-term strategy.
